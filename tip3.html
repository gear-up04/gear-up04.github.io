How To Choose The Right Oil For Your Vehicle

Engine oil enables your vehicle to run smoothly, and choosing the oil for your engine may seem a challenging task. There is a lot of information regarding various engine oil choices, but the first thing you need to do is check your vehicle's manual. It lists the recommended oil weight, standard format, and viscosity grade. You need to have a proper understanding of the importance of engine oil to enable you to choose the type of oil for your engine. This article breaks down various choices to assist you in the expedition to change your oil.

Engine Oil Type

The last aspect to consider when choosing the right oil for your vehicle is the type of engine oil. However, if you understand the model and make of your car, it is easy to make an oil type selection decision. Check your vehicle's manual for proper guidance to know what your engine requires. It is also essential to understand the primary differences between different engine oil such as mineral oil, full and semi-synthetic oils:

Mineral Oils:

This type of oil has a comparatively low resistance to oxidation and heat. It can sustain your vehicle for approximately 5000 kilometers without the need to change it regularly, thus saving you extra money. If your car engine is old and always uses mineral oil, it will give it additional protection and performance.

Synthetic Oil

Synthetic oil is modified chemically to enhance engine performance and contain very few impurities. Synthetic oils are of high quality because they have high resistance to oxidation and heat. Your car can last approximately 10000 kilometers before changing the oil. Synthetic oils enhance engine performance and prolong its life. This type of oil economies on fuel and has perfect anti-friction.
